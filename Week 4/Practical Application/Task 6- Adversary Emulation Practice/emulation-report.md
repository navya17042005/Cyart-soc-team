The adversary emulation simulated a phishing attack (MITRE ATT&CK T1566) using a PowerShell-based payload. The activity successfully triggered detection mechanisms, identifying suspicious script execution and abnormal network connections. Alerts were generated, indicating effective monitoring of endpoint behavior. However, detection relied primarily on execution-based indicators, highlighting a gap in early-stage email filtering and initial access detection. Improved email security controls and enhanced threat intelligence integration could strengthen early detection. Overall, the SOC demonstrated the ability to detect and respond to post-exploitation activity, but improvements are required in proactive threat prevention and initial attack vector identification.
